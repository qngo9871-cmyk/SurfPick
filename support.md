# Surf Pick — Support

For help, feedback, or to report a bug, email **[qngo9871@gmail.com](mailto:qngo9871@gmail.com?subject=Surf%20Pick%20support)**. Replies usually within a few days.

## Common questions

### Why does Surf Pick need my location?
To find the surf breaks closest to where you are. Your location is used only on your device — it is never sent to a server, never stored, never shared. See the [Privacy Policy](/SurfPick/privacy) for details.

### A spot is in the wrong place. How do I report it?
Tap the spot on the main list to open the detail screen, then tap **"Report wrong location"** at the bottom. This opens an email with the spot name and its current coordinates pre-filled — paste the correct coordinates from Google Maps or Apple Maps and send. Reports go directly to the developer and feed into the next dataset update.

### How accurate are the conditions?
Wave height, period, direction, and wind data come from [Open-Meteo's marine forecast](https://open-meteo.com/marine). Forecasts are model-based estimates — always trust your eyes when you arrive at the spot.

### Why a green / amber / red rating instead of a score out of 10?
A traffic-light rating answers the question you actually have: *should I drive there?* A 7.4/10 score doesn't.

### What does Pro unlock?
- All 10 nearest spots (free shows the top 3)
- Home-screen widget with your top pick
- Auto-CarPlay widget on iOS 26

Pro is a one-time payment. There is no subscription.

### How do I restore my Pro purchase on a new device?
Open Settings → tap **Restore Purchases**. As long as you're signed in to the same Apple ID, your Pro unlock is restored.

### Does Surf Pick work without an internet connection?
You need a connection to fetch live conditions for nearby breaks. The last successful fetch is cached, so the app opens with your most recent data even when offline.

### Does Surf Pick work outside Australia?
Yes — the bundled spot dataset covers surf breaks worldwide. Coverage in some regions is sparser; if a known break is missing, please email the developer with the spot name and coordinates.
